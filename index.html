Technology IP live production

Sony is actively developing IP-based approaches for professional video production, and has created a new Networked Media interface. This packetises video, audio, and metadata, enabling real-time transmission between equipment via standard network infrastructures.

This approach combines the latest IP network technologies with the current SDI standard interface for any resolution of video transmission. While conventional systems require multiple different types of cable to carry various signals (video, audio, reference, metadata, and control data), our new approach requires only a single standard network cable passing through conventional network switches.

Our new technology supports the direct connection of numerous types of production device onto the IP network. It also enables simultaneous transmission to multiple points and real-time noiseless source switching, handling SD, HD, and 4K video resolutions together.

Customer story: internation crijket council

Pulselive is the official digital partner of the International Cricket Council, responsible for all of their digital and mobile properties including the ICC Cricket World Cup.

“In today’s digital world, it’s essential for any sport’s governing body to embrace the opportunity that digital offers. As the ICC’s digital partner Pulselive is an extension to our team. Pulselive’s comprehensive knowledge of the industry combined with their pro-active approach, creative flair and technical ability enables the ICC to achieve its digital objectives. Pulselive are quick to accommodate our evolving requirements always putting the ICC’s interests first.”

Aarti Dabas, Head of Media Rights, Broadcast and Digital, ICC

Insight
Pulselive works closely with the ICC and their commercial partners to create and deliver a digital and social strategy which fulfils their objective of ‘capitalising on digital to create value for the ICC and their stakeholders’. Our remit also extends to broadcast and in-venue integration and identifying and executing fan engagement opportunities across multiple touch points.
Technology
We’ve developed a new digital platform for the ICC which has consolidated all of their tournament sites under one domain, a process which involved significant content migration work. The new platform is home to all the ICC’s major tournament sites including the ICC Cricket World Cup, ICC World T20, ICC Champions Trophy and the ICC U19 Cricket World Cup. We also provide managed SEO services, which have seen the ICC’s organic search impressions increase by 178% in the last 12 months.
UX and design
Using the ICC’s corporate and event brand guidelines, Pulselive created a consistent user interface that flows through all of the ICC’s digital properties. We developed a cross-platform flexible UI toolkit and template structure which can accommodate multiple pages and content requirements and by taking a mobile-first approach, the user experience is seamless, from mobile, to tablet, to desktop.
Social media
Pulselive is responsible for planning and implementing the ICC’s social media strategy with the core goal of promoting the global game and driving referrals to the ICC’s digital properties. We work closely with the ICC to plan, manage and execute a content strategy across their social media properties, with the added benefit that the ICC benefit from Pulselive’s relationships with all of the major social media networks.
Live content
As the digital division of Hawk-Eye, Pulselive has access to huge volumes of data. We’ve developed visually enhanced live match centre and scoring services as well as engaging analytical graphics that sit alongside blogs, editorial and VOD content to deliver immersive experiences for the ICC’s audiences. We also worked alongside Hawk-Eye to produce and stream live World T20 and Cricket World Cup qualifiers.
